We, just like you, want to learn more about embedded systems and keep mastering our craft.

But, over months or years, we faced the frustration of reading not at all easy to understand tutorials or books.

Just to mention some...

Problem 1: Abuse of passive voice:


Please read the following sentences.

1) "This feature CAN BE USED to read the contents of a characteristic value by using its handle."
2) "With this feature you can use characteristic's handle to read its the contents."

Which one was easier for you to understand?

The number 2, right?

The reason is that in the passive voice we aren't sure who or what is doing the action. That's why it's easier to understand sentences in the active voice.

Problem 2: Knowledge gaps:


Imagine this situation. You were installing an update on your laptop but suddenly your battery run out of battery and your laptop shut down.

When you turned it on you read the following message:
error: file /boot/vmlinuz-5.1-x86_64 not found

The next thing you do is google the error. You ended up one forum that tells you

You have to chroot your system and from there you mount your kernel image"
-- somebody in manjaro forum --


It's a good answer. But you are still not sure what you need to do because you don't know what chroot is. Or you don't know how to find your kernel image or how to mount it.

And... let's be honest

When we google our problem we expect a step-by-step or how-to answer.

For example, it would have been cooler if that guy would have written a summary of this post or at least added also the link to it.
